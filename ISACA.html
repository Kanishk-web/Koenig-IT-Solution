History of ISACA

### History


ISACA , originally known as the Information Systems Audit and Control Association, has been a beacon of progress in the IT governance space since its inception in 1969. This
global association was established by a group of like-minded individuals seeking to create a centralized source of information and guidance in the IT audit and control industry.
Over the years, ISACA has developed into a pioneering entity , setting standards with its COBIT framework for IT management and governance.

The organization's dedication to innovation is evident through its contributions to cyber security with the Cybersecurity Nexus (CSX) program and the CMMI (Capability Maturity
Model Integration). ISACA's certification programs, like the CISA , CISM , CGEIT , and CRISC , have become gold standards for IT professionals aiming to excel in their careers.

Interesting facts about ISACA include its global reach, impacting over 140 countries, and a membership of more than 145,000 professionals. This reflects its role in shaping and
supporting the IT industry and fostering a community dedicated to knowledge sharing, advocacy, and professional excellence.
